# Style Axis And Adjustment Tradeoffs

Use this reference to translate user adjustment directions into concrete art changes.

## Common Style Axes

| Axis | Move Toward A | Move Toward B | Typical Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| Age impression | younger, cuter | older, cooler | younger improves accessibility; older may improve depth but reduce warmth |
| Detail density | cleaner | richer | cleaner improves readability; richer improves premium feel but raises cost |
| Shape language | rounder | sharper | rounder feels friendly; sharper feels tense or mature |
| Color saturation | softer | stronger | softer feels gentle; stronger increases attention but can fatigue |
| Lightness | brighter | darker | brighter supports casual appeal; darker supports drama but can hurt clarity |
| Material | flat | glossy/volumetric | flat is efficient; glossy feels polished but needs consistency |
| UI depth | flat | raised/beveled | flat feels modern; raised improves clickability |
| Genre tone | playful | strategic | playful broadens appeal; strategic may need more controlled visual hierarchy |
| Market position | familiar | differentiated | familiar lowers learning cost; differentiated reduces collision risk |

## Adjustment Evaluation Format

For each user direction, write:

```text
Adjustment direction:
Visual axis affected:
Potential benefit:
Potential risk:
Tradeoff:
Recommended compromise:
Screenshots supporting the judgment:
```

## Frequent Direction Translations

### "More Premium"

Usually means:

- tighter palette
- lower color noise
- more controlled lighting
- more material consistency
- cleaner UI spacing
- fewer cheap-looking gradients

Risk:

- may lose casual immediacy
- may increase art cost
- can reduce reward excitement if over-muted

### "More Cute"

Usually means:

- rounder shape language
- larger eyes or softer facial features
- higher lightness
- warmer accents
- lower sharpness
- smaller threat cues

Risk:

- may weaken competitive or strategic tone
- may become too close to children's toy aesthetics

### "More Clear"

Usually means:

- stronger foreground/background separation
- fewer decorative details near gameplay objects
- clearer icon silhouettes
- more consistent functional color use
- reduced texture noise

Risk:

- may feel plain if material richness is removed too much

### "More Differentiated"

Usually means:

- move away from the competitor's most recognizable combined traits
- keep only abstract category conventions
- change at least two of: palette, shape language, material, UI panel grammar, theme, camera

Risk:

- can drift away from proven category expectations
- may need more validation with target players

### "More Casual"

Usually means:

- brighter palette
- simpler silhouettes
- stronger CTA colors
- lower detail density
- friendlier shapes
- less realistic material

Risk:

- can feel less premium
- may reduce long-term depth impression

### "More Mature"

Usually means:

- more restrained palette
- lower saturation
- sharper shape accents
- more controlled lighting
- more realistic material hints

Risk:

- may reduce accessibility and quick readability
- can conflict with light casual gameplay

## Differentiation Rule

When avoiding collision with a competitor, change at least two major visual layers:

- color structure
- shape language
- material/light treatment
- UI component grammar
- theme or motif
- composition and density

Do not claim a direction is differentiated if it only changes small details while retaining the same palette, shape language, UI hierarchy, and subject motifs.
